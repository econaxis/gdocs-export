I think the most despicable character in The Canterbury Tales would be the Prioress. While the description of her mostly paints a clean picture of the Prioress as a gentle, loving princess, a closer inspection reveals that the Prioress’s “courtly” behaviour is nothing more than a mask. The lines “She wolde weepe if that she saw a mous,” and “hir over-lippe wiped she so clene/that in hir coppe ther was no ferthing seene” reiterate her perfect manners and gentle character. It seems as if, throughout the whole description, the Prioress lacks any despicable qualities—she is perfect in almost every way. However, that seeming “perfectness” is what makes her despicable.
